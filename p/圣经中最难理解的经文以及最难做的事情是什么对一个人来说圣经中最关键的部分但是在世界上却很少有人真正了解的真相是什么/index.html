圣经中最难理解的经文，以及最难做的事情是什么。对一个人来说，圣经中最关键的部分，但是在世界上却很少有人真正了解的真相是什么

Mar 08, 2025

One of the most challenging aspects of the Bible lies in its layers of symbolism and profound demands on personal transformation. Many readers and scholars find the apocalyptic literature—especially the vivid imagery in the Book of Revelation—difficult to interpret. These passages, full of symbolic numbers, beasts, and visions, often leave even seasoned theologians with more questions than answers. For example:

ESV: “Then I saw a beast rising out of the sea, with ten horns and seven heads…” (Revelation 13:1, ESV)
和合本: “我又看见一只兽从海中上来，有十个角，七个头…"（启示录 13:1，和合本）

At the same time, the Bible challenges believers with ethical imperatives that defy our natural instincts. One of the hardest commands is found in Jesus’ teaching to love not only our friends but also our enemies. This call to unconditional love and forgiveness demands a radical self-sacrifice and humility that many find extremely difficult to practice. For instance:

ESV: “But I say to you, Love your enemies and pray for those who persecute you.” (Matthew 5:44, ESV)
和合本: “只是我告诉你们：要爱你们的仇敌，为那逼迫你们的祷告。"（马太福音 5:44，和合本）

Beyond individual commands or symbolic texts, the Bible’s most crucial truth—the central message that transforms lives—is the gospel of grace and redemption through Jesus Christ. Despite being the heart of the Christian faith, this truth is often misunderstood or overlooked in a world that prizes self-reliance and personal merit. The Bible teaches that salvation is not earned by human effort but is a gift of God’s unmerited favor. In this light, the key message is that true transformation occurs when a person accepts God’s love and allows that grace to redefine every aspect of their life. Consider the call to self-denial and the invitation to follow Christ wholeheartedly:

ESV: “If anyone would come after me, let him deny himself and take up his cross daily and follow me.” (Luke 9:23, ESV)
和合本: “若有人要跟从我，就当舍己，天天背起他的十字架来跟从我。"（路加福音 9:23，和合本）

In summary, while the Bible contains many passages that are intellectually and spiritually challenging—from the enigmatic visions of Revelation to the seemingly impossible demands of unconditional love—the most critical and transformative truth is the message of grace. It is this truth, that our relationship with God is founded on His love and mercy rather than our own deeds, that remains at the heart of Christian teaching, yet is fully grasped by only a few in its profound depth and radical call to life transformation.
